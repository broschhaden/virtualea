Service & Retainer Agreement

This Service & Retainer Agreement is made between the parties named below. It sets out the basis on which Rosch Haden will provide executive support services to the Client.

1. Scope of services

The Provider will provide remote executive support, executive partnership, coordination, prioritisation, operational structure, follow-through, meeting preparation, project momentum and related business support as agreed in writing with the Client.

The agreed scope may be set out in this agreement, an accepted proposal, statement of work, email confirmation or retainer schedule. If there is any conflict, the most recent written scope agreed by both parties will apply.

Unless expressly agreed in writing, the Provider is not responsible for execution outcomes, operational delivery by third parties, business performance, financial results, legal compliance of the Client’s business, or decisions made by the Client.

2. Out of scope work

Work outside the agreed scope, additional hours, urgent work, new projects, travel, in-person support, specialist advisory work or material changes to responsibilities may be quoted and charged separately. The Provider is not obliged to carry out out-of-scope work unless agreed in writing.

3. Hours, availability and working arrangements

Services will be provided remotely unless otherwise agreed. Any estimated hours, response times or working patterns are guidance only unless expressly stated as binding in writing.

  • Retainer support is subject to reasonable availability and agreed priorities.
  • Unused time does not roll over unless expressly agreed in writing.
  • Urgent or priority work may require separate agreement.
  • The Client must provide timely access to information, systems, decisions and approvals required for the Provider to perform the services.

4. Fees, invoicing and payment

The Client will pay the agreed fees in accordance with the payment terms set out above or otherwise confirmed in writing. Unless agreed otherwise, monthly retainers are payable in advance.

  • Invoices must be paid by the due date stated on the invoice.
  • Late payment may result in services being paused, withheld or terminated.
  • The Provider may charge statutory interest and reasonable debt recovery costs under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 where applicable.
  • Fees are exclusive of expenses, third-party costs and taxes unless stated otherwise.
  • The Client remains liable for all fees incurred up to the effective termination date.

5. Expenses and third-party costs

The Client will reimburse any pre-approved expenses and third-party costs reasonably incurred in providing the services. The Provider will not commit to material third-party costs without prior written approval where reasonably practicable.

6. Client responsibilities

The Client remains responsible for all business decisions, approvals, instructions, operations, legal obligations, governance, internal controls and outcomes. The Client must ensure that information supplied to the Provider is accurate, lawful and complete.

7. No employment, partnership or agency

The Provider acts as an independent contractor. Nothing in this agreement creates an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, fiduciary relationship or agency relationship. The Provider has no authority to bind the Client, enter into contracts, approve expenditure or make final decisions on behalf of the Client unless expressly authorised in writing.

8. Confidentiality

Each party must keep confidential all non-public commercial, financial, operational, strategic, technical, client, employee and business information received from the other party. Confidential information may only be used for the purposes of discussing, preparing or performing the services.

Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that is already public, lawfully known before disclosure, independently developed, or required to be disclosed by law, court order, regulator or professional adviser.

9. Data protection

Each party will comply with applicable UK data protection laws, including UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Where the Provider processes personal data on behalf of the Client, the parties will cooperate in good faith to put appropriate data processing terms in place where required.

The Client is responsible for ensuring it has a lawful basis to share personal data with the Provider and that any instructions involving personal data are lawful.

10. Intellectual property

All pre-existing materials, methods, processes, frameworks, templates, know-how, trade secrets and working practices belonging to the Provider remain the property of the Provider.

Client-specific deliverables created specifically for the Client will transfer to the Client upon full payment of all related fees, except for Provider background materials, know-how, templates and reusable methods, which remain the Provider’s property. The Client receives a licence to use any embedded Provider materials internally for its own business purposes.

11. Non-solicitation

During the agreement and for 12 months after it ends, the Client must not knowingly solicit or engage any subcontractor, supplier or associate introduced by the Provider in connection with the services, except with the Provider’s written consent.

12. No guarantee

The Provider will perform the services with reasonable care and skill. However, the Provider does not guarantee any particular commercial, operational, strategic, financial or personal outcome. Results depend on factors outside the Provider’s control, including Client decisions, resources, implementation and third-party performance.

13. Limitation of liability

Nothing in this agreement limits or excludes liability where it would be unlawful to do so, including liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation.

Subject to the above, to the fullest extent permitted by law:

  • the Provider’s total aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this agreement is capped at the total fees paid by the Client to the Provider in the 3 months immediately before the event giving rise to the claim;
  • the Provider is not liable for indirect, consequential or special loss;
  • the Provider is not liable for loss of profit, revenue, business, opportunity, goodwill, anticipated savings, data, contracts or reputation;
  • the Provider is not liable for losses caused by Client instructions, Client decisions, inaccurate information, third-party systems, third-party providers, delays outside the Provider’s control, or unauthorised use of deliverables.

14. Indemnity

The Client agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Provider against claims, losses, damages, costs and expenses arising from the Client’s business decisions, operations, unlawful instructions, breach of this agreement, misuse of deliverables, infringement of third-party rights, or information supplied by the Client.

15. Suspension

The Provider may suspend services immediately if invoices are overdue, instructions are unlawful or unreasonable, required information is not provided, the Client breaches confidentiality, or continuing work would expose the Provider to legal, financial, ethical or reputational risk.

16. Termination

Either party may terminate this agreement by giving 30 days’ written notice unless a different notice period is agreed in writing. Fees remain payable during the notice period.

The Provider may terminate immediately if the Client materially breaches this agreement, fails to pay overdue sums, acts unlawfully, requests unethical work, misuses confidential information, or behaves in a way that makes the working relationship unsafe, untenable or commercially unreasonable.

17. Effects of termination

On termination, the Client must pay all outstanding invoices, fees, expenses and approved third-party costs. The Provider will provide a reasonable handover of work completed up to termination where appropriate and where all undisputed sums have been paid.

Clauses relating to confidentiality, intellectual property, payment, liability, indemnity, data protection and governing law survive termination.

18. Force majeure

Neither party will be liable for delay or failure to perform obligations caused by events outside reasonable control, including illness, power failure, internet failure, cyber incident, industrial action, extreme weather, legal restrictions or third-party platform failure.

19. Notices

Formal notices must be sent by email or in writing to the contact details most recently provided by each party. Notices by email are deemed received on the next business day unless a bounce-back or delivery failure is received.

20. Entire agreement and variations

This agreement, together with any accepted proposal, statement of work or written scope confirmation, forms the entire agreement between the parties and replaces prior discussions. Changes must be agreed in writing.

21. Severability

If any part of this agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions will continue in force.

22. Assignment and subcontracting

The Client may not assign this agreement without the Provider’s written consent. The Provider may use carefully selected subcontractors or professional advisers where reasonably required, while remaining responsible for the services provided directly to the Client.

23. Governing law and jurisdiction

This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction over disputes arising out of or in connection with this agreement, unless the parties agree another dispute resolution process in writing.

24. Acceptance and signatures

By signing below, the parties confirm that they have authority to enter into this agreement and agree to be bound by its terms.
